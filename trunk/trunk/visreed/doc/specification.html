Regular Expression Tree

Regular Expression can be represented and constructed in a form of a tree (an "ordered, rooted, directed tree").

Particularly, this is a tree with only nodes (no edges).

Regular Expression Nodes

There are many kinds of regular expression nodes, as listed below:

Table 1   Regular Expression Nodes
NameNotationDescription
Sequence';'Matches the sequence made of its children
Alternation'|'Matches any one of its children
Kleene Star'*'0 or more times of repetition of its child
Kleene Plus'+'1 or more times of repetition of its child
TerminalTerminal characters

Normalization of Regular Expressions

In order to simplify the complexity of regular expressions, a suggested normal form named sequential normal form is represented by the following rules:

  • The root of the tree is always a sequence node
  • Sequence nodes may have 0 or more children, but none can be a sequence node
  • Alternation nodes must have 2 or more children, all must be sequence nodes
  • Kleene star nodes must have exactly 1 child and it must be a sequence node
  • Kleene plus nodes must have exactly 1 child and it must be a sequence node
  • Terminal nodes must have no children

The above rules gives us some facts:

  • The root of the tree is always a sequence node
  • The branche nodes of the tree may be sequence, alternation, kleene star or kleene plus nodes
  • A leaf nodes is either a sequence node or a terminal node
  • Sequence nodes only appears on the same level of a tree, and these levels appears in alternation.
    (e.g. sequence nodes can never appear in the level of direct children of the root node)

Notations of the Regular Expression Nodes

Currently there is no particular notations for lexical nodes of regular expressions. These notations are only for temporary use and subject to change later.

  • All nodes in lexical tree diagram are shown as a circle, with an abbreviation in the center.
