Although the practice assignment will not be marked, you should submit it anyway. This is to make sure the system is working with and for you. Even a minor mistake in the submission process can cause your assignment to fail completely.

Submission of Assignments

All assignments will be submitted via WebSubmit.

You may submit multiple times prior to the deadline. Only the last submission will be saved. Please make sure the assignment compiles and runs properly before you submit. Last minute cosmetic changes have been behind more students assignments failing to compile than any other three causes put together.

Marking

Assignments are marked by an autograder which automatically compiles and tests your assignment. If you do not follow the assignment directions quite precisely, you are likely to end up getting a zero because it will fail to compile. Sorry about that, but we handle about 2,000 assignments each year making human intervention very expensive. If you do get 0 for your masterpiece, don't panic! There are remedies.

Each assignment is marked out of 10 points so all assignments are weighted equally. Your worst assignment is automatically dropped to allow for sickness, funerals or simply an off week. Thus you're total assignment mark is out of 42. It will be worth 12% of your final grade.

Resubmits

If you get 0 on your assignment, don't panic! As long as you submitted an assignment on time in the first place, you will be allowed a resubmit (one). For assignments 4-8, one point will be deducted for a resubmit. You receive the maximum of the two marks.

Group Work

Group work is not permitted. You must do your own assignments and any violation of this will be treated as plagiarism.

Due Date

All assignments are due 2 days after your lab, by 8:50 am. Late submissions are not accepted except by special permission of the instructor.

Lab/Tutorials

Lab/tutorials are held in the main computer lab (EN3000). They are designed to help you work on your assignments. As they are only an hour long you are strongly advised to review and make a start on your assignment before going to the lab.
